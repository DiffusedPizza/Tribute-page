Roy P. Benavidez

Legend

"Tango Mike Mike"

Here is a brief timeline on the life of
Master Sergeant Benavidez

  • 1935 - Born in Cuero, Texas
  • 1952 - Benavidez enlists in the Texas Army National Guard during the Korean War
  • 1955 - Switches from the Army National Guard to Army active duty
  • 1959 - Married Hilaria Coy Benavidez, completed Airborne training and was assigned to the 82nd Airborne Division at Fort Bragg, North Carolina
  • 1965 - He gets sent to South Vietnam as a Special Forces advisor to an Army of the Republic of Vietnam regiment. During his tour of duty he stepped on a land mine.
  • 1966 - after over a year of hospitalization, Benavidez walked out of the hospital with his wife at his side, determined to return to combat in Vietnam.
  • 1968 - May 2nd would be a day of chaos for Detachment B-56. The tragic events of this day would stay with these men for the rest of their lives. The heroic actions of Benavidez would earn him his Medal of Honor. You can read a detailed account of the events here.
  • 1976 - Benavidez retires from his military career. He spent the next several years traveling the country as a guest speaker. He made encouraging and guiding young americans his priority, particularly pushing underprivileged children away from gangs and into military service, to bring productivity and purpose.
  • 1981 - President Ronald Reagan presents Roy P. Benavidez
    with the Medal of Honor in the Pentagon
  • 1983 - He fought for his Vietnam War comrades one last time, successfully advocating for the continuation of disability benefits for Vietnam veterans.
  • 1998 - Roy Benavidez passes away November 29th at the age of 63 and was buried at Fort Sam Houston National Cemetery in San Antonio, Texas.
